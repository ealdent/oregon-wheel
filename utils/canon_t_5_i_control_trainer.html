How to use this page

Choose a shooting mode in the sticky bar, then click a control on the camera. The right panel tells you what that control does in the selected mode.

Start with M if you want full manual control. Start with Av if you want the useful everyday “make the background blurry or sharp” mode.

    2. Manual mode

    M mode: three knobs and one meter.

    Manual mode is not mystical. It is just this: shutter speed controls time, aperture controls the size of the lens opening, ISO controls amplification, and the meter tells you whether the camera thinks the result is too dark or bright.

    Reference

    Small glossary, large payoff.

    Stop

    A doubling or halving of light. 1/125 to 1/250 is one stop darker. ISO 400 to 800 is one stop brighter.

    Shutter speed

    How long the sensor sees light. Faster freezes motion but darkens the image. Slower brightens but blurs movement and hand shake.

    Aperture

    The lens opening. Lower f-number means more light and shallower depth of field. Higher f-number means less light and deeper focus.

    ISO

    Signal amplification. Higher ISO brightens the file but adds noise and reduces clean detail.

    Exposure compensation

    In P, Tv, and Av, this tells the camera to make the automatic exposure brighter or darker than its meter’s normal.

    AE lock

    Freezes the camera’s metered auto exposure so you can recompose without the exposure changing.

    AF operation

    One-Shot for still subjects, AI Servo for moving subjects, AI Focus lets the camera switch automatically.

    Metering

    How the camera evaluates brightness: whole scene, center-weighted, partial, or spot. The meter is a recommendation, not a court order.
